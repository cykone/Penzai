Meet Sam a social media specialist from London

Transcript

Who are you and what are you doing?

I am Sam I am 28 yearsold and I work for a big social media agency in London. We specialise in PR and Communications for loads of consumer clients.

How does your regular work day look like?

An average day in my life is getting up heading to the office. First thing in the morning, I am checking my emails, making sure I am prioritised for the day. And then it is usually a lot of meetings with my internal team and also... usually working on big strategy documents or meeting with production companies to make sure the content that we are producing aligns to the client brief.

What do you like about your job?

The thing that I am absolutly loving about my job is seeing a project from start to finish. Because we work with this content and the production process for so long, it is really satisfying seeing something from a piece of paper to actually going online. Another thing that is also really great is that when that piece of content gets live that you can physically see how many people consumed that. As opposed to a piece of press that ends in a newspaper.

What don't you like about it?

The thing that I disslike about my job is the lack of creativity at times. This is purely because our clients have a commercial goal. So sometimes that means your creativity and wacky ideas can't come to consideration. You have always to be mindful of the clients needs.

What are the core skills to do you job?

Some of the core skills that you need to do this job is the ability to mulittask because you are often work for two or three clients at the time, so the ability to stay on top is key. That leads nicly to the second core skill that you need, being organised. It is really really important that you are always on top of everything.

What would you recommend someone who wants to work in your field?

For anybody starting in this industry, I think it is really important that you really throw yourself in the deep end as much as possible. So whatever if it internships or getting as much hands-on experience as possible. As long as you are possitiv and you are showing willingness in all the areas that you are in. It is really the best way to get as much experience as possible to define what you are good at and what your core skills are
